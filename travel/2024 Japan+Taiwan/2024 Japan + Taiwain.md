### 大竹祭 (Otake festival) 2024/10/20
In Oct every year Otake has their city level festival.  Naoko's mother spent the day helping out with food and work. In the morning the children helped pull the neighborhood Mikosho, a small shinto palanquin, around the neighborhood.  It's the size of 4 microwaves.  In the past the kids volunteered, now they must be bride with 2,000円 gift to spend at the festival stalls. Much longer ago, the stalls were staffed by local vendors.  Now there are vendors who travel from festival to festival selling things. 

The streets where lined with strings with paper Ofuda, shinto talismans, hanging from them for good luck.

We watched the parade.  At the front of the parade come two Chinese dragons.  If they bite your head you will have good luck for one year. Lucky for me, my head looked tasty.  After this came the Sankin Kotai, the traditional transfer of power from one daimyo to another. Men marched in cosplay of traditional closes and the "daimyo" came marking on a horse. 

Then came a series of mikosho from each neighborhood.  Men marching in white clothes where 40, traditional time for in shinto for a middle life crises,  They would dance with the mikosho to help them get passed it.

A man with the mikosho just after naoko's asked me to help carry it.  I remember my pledge to say "yes", and found myself "fighting"/carrying/dancing against naoko's neighborhood.  One of naoko's team fell, so we must have won.

Some of them were much larger then others. Wonders if there is a sponering business.  It looked like there was as many people in the parade as watching it.
