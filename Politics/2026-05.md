### 1. Steve Hilton (Republican)
- **Background:** A British-American political commentator, former Fox News host, and Silicon Valley tech entrepreneur. He previously served as a senior adviser to former UK Prime Minister David Cameron.
- **Platform:** Backed by Donald Trump, Hilton leads much of the Republican field. His platform leans heavily into deregulation and cost-cutting: he wants to suspend state environmental regulations to lower gas prices, slash income taxes for middle-class and wealthy earners, and open up natural spaces to build suburban single-family homes.
- **Polling Status:** Currently leading or tied for the top spot, averaging around 16% to 17% support.
### 2. Tom Steyer (Democratic)
- **Background:** A billionaire activist and philanthropist who made his fortune managing a hedge fund before pivoting to environmental advocacy and a 2020 presidential run.
- **Platform:** Steyer is running a massive self-funded campaign, spending upwards of $132 million to flood the airwaves. He’s promising a 25% cut to electricity bills, alongside aggressive climate initiatives.However, his opponents frequently target his past financial investments in fossil fuels and private prisons.
- **Polling Status:** Running neck-and-neck at the top of the field with roughly 14% to 15% support.
### 3. Xavier Becerra (Democratic)

- **Background:** A titan of California and federal politics, Becerra has served as the U.S. Secretary of Health and Human Services, California Attorney General, and a long-time member of Congress representing Los Angeles.
    
- **Platform:** Becerra heavily leans into his record of suing the first Trump administration over 100 times. His platform centers on expanding Medi-Cal toward a single-payer healthcare system, addressing the cost of living by capping utility and insurance rates, and targeting corporate price gouging. He has recently had to fend off debate attacks regarding a fraud case involving a former top political adviser.
    
- **Polling Status:** Hovering between 10% and 13% after seeing a spring surge following Congressman Eric Swalwell's exit from the race.
    

### 4. Chad Bianco (Republican)

- **Background:** The current Riverside County Sheriff who has spent three decades in law enforcement. He was first elected to the position in 2018 with heavy backing from deputy unions.
    
- **Platform:** Bianco is running on an uncompromising law-and-order and fiscal conservative platform. He wants to completely overturn California's sanctuary state laws, boost local oil and gas production, suspend environmental rules, and entirely eliminate both the state income tax and the gas tax.
    
- **Polling Status:** Holding steady at 10% to 14% support, drawing a solid base of conservative and law-enforcement-aligned voters.
    

### 5. Katie Porter (Democratic)

- **Background:** A UC Irvine law professor specializing in consumer protection and a former U.S. Representative who famously flipped a red Orange County congressional seat in 2018.
    
- **Platform:** Renowned for using a whiteboard to aggressively grill corporate executives during congressional hearings, Porter markets herself as a populist progressive who is "not for sale" to corporate donors. She wants to raise taxes on large corporations while cutting income taxes for middle-class families, and supports developing dense urban housing near transit hubs.
    
- **Polling Status:** Sitting at around 9% to 10% in recent surveys, maintaining a dedicated base of progressive voters and working parents.
