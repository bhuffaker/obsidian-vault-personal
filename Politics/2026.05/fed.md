###  Tom Umberg (Democratic)
- **Summary:** A powerhouse in California politics, Umberg is a sitting State Senator and former U.S. Army Colonel. His massive fundraising lead—nearly eight times his closest competitor—combined with high name recognition makes him the frontrunner.
- **Platform:** Focuses on transparency in tax assessments and utilizing his legislative experience to streamline board operations.
- **Supports:** Backed by major labor unions including **SEIU California**, the **California Teachers Association**, and **Equality California**.
- **History:** Long history of public service, including terms in the State Assembly and Senate, and as a federal prosecutor.
### **Martin Arias (Democratic)**
- **Summary:** Arias is positioning himself as the technical expert in the race. He currently works as a taxpayer advocate in the San Diego County Assessor’s Office, giving him direct "on-the-ground" experience with BOE matters.
- **Platform:** Emphasizes protecting homeowners from unfair assessments and modernizing the taxpayer appeals process.
- **Supports:** Co-endorsed by the **San Diego County Democratic Party**.
- **History:** Decades of experience within county tax administration, serving as a non-partisan expert before entering this race.
### **Denis Bilodeau (Republican)**
- **Summary:** As the only high-profile Republican in a district that still holds significant conservative pockets (Orange and Riverside counties), Bilodeau is a strong contender to make the "Top Two" runoff.
- **Platform:** Taxpayer protection, opposing "hidden" tax increases, and fiscal accountability.
- **Supports:** Endorsed by the **California Republican Party**, **Reform California**, and the **Howard Jarvis Taxpayers Association**.
- **History:** An Orange City Councilmember and Orange County Water District board member with deep roots in local GOP politics.

### Brian Goldsmith (SD-24 - Los Angeles)
- **Funding:** Currently one of the most well-funded candidates in the state, bolstered by significant personal seeding and high-profile donors.
- **Likelihood to Win:** Very High. Running in a safe Democratic district for a seat vacated by Ben Allen.
- **Platform & History:** A small business owner with deep ties to the Los Angeles Jewish community. His platform focuses on pragmatism in business, enhancing public safety, and mental health services.
- **Key Supports:** California Association of Highway Patrolmen, California Legislative Jewish Caucus, and various business labor groups.
### Catherine Blakespear (SD-38 - San Diego/Orange Co.)
- **Funding:** Strong fundraising base with over $750,000 reported.
- **Likelihood to Win:** High. Though the district has a "competitive" lean, she is the incumbent and has the full weight of the state party behind her.
- **Platform & History:** Former Mayor of Encinitas. She focuses heavily on climate change, specifically coastal preservation and wildfire prevention.
- **Key Supports:** California Democratic Party, SEIU California, and California Professional Firefighters.
### Esmeralda Soria (SD-14 - Central Valley)
- **Funding:** As a sitting Assemblymember moving to the Senate, she has access to a massive established donor network and "frontrunner" momentum.
- **Likelihood to Win:** High. While the district is trending rightward, she is the clear Democratic favorite.
- **Platform & History:** A former law professor and Fresno City Councilmember. Her platform is centered on the "Valley's needs"—water security, rural healthcare, and agricultural labor rights.
- **Key Supports:** California Democratic Party, AFSCME, and SEIU California.
