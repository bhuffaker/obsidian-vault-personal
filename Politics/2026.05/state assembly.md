#### **Jodi Cleesattle**
- **History:** Cleesattle is a seasoned litigator with the California Department of Justice. She has served as a Senior Assistant Attorney General, overseeing large teams of attorneys. She is also a past president of the Tom Homann LGBTQ+ Law Association and has been active in the San Diego County Bar Association.
- **Platform:** Her campaign emphasizes **judicial temperament** and **extensive experience** in both state and federal courts. She frames herself as a candidate who understands the complexities of the law from a high-level administrative and trial perspective.
- **Likelihood Factors:** Candidates with "Attorney General" or "Judge Pro Tem" in their titles historically perform very well in California judicial races. She is viewed as the "institutional" choice.
#### **Adam Noakes**
- **History:** Noakes is a career prosecutor with the San Diego County District Attorney’s Office. He has spent years handling criminal cases, ranging from misdemeanors to serious felonies.
- **Platform:** His platform is rooted in **public safety**. He emphasizes his front-line experience in the courtroom dealing with criminal justice and advocates for a court system that prioritizes the protection of the community.
- **Likelihood Factors:** Noakes is endorsed by **Reform California** (Carl DeMaio’s organization), which provides him with a dedicated block of conservative and "tough-on-crime" voters. In a primary, this specific mobilization can be a powerful equalizer against a better-funded institutional candidate.
